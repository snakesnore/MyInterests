My Interests

One could say that I have many different interests from all kinds of fields. I enjoy art, science and even some sports. This web page was created to document my interests. If you want to know why I started learning how to program, then click here.

Art

My art-loving journey began when I was rather young. At three years old my parents bought me my first sketchbook and while I didn't fill it up with masterpieces, it gave me a good base for learning and made me realise that art was something I was interested in.

Drawing

Painting
At first I used pencils and crayons for drawing. This method has stuck with me since and whenever I don't have paints at hand, I use lead pencils or nowadays even pens to casually sketch on the go. My first paints were basic gouache paints found in every kindergarten but because of my negative experiences with them I thought that I didn't like paints. Not until a few years later when I discovered watercolors did I realise how fun paints could actually be. Nowadays I use gouache, watercolor and occasionally even acrylics and oils.

How to start your own art journey

  1. Get a paper and pen
  2. Start doodling
  3. Don't take yourself too seriously
  4. Keep practising, if you stop for any reason then analyse why
  5. Don't compare yourself to other people
  6. Look up tutorials
  7. When in need of motivation, compare your older drawings with newer works

Books

Books hold a great deal of importance to me. I started reading when I was five, so not that early, but when I was six, I already read rather advanced books, like Tarzan. My kindergarten teacher was rather impressed but the truth was that I couldn't really comprehend the difficult text even if I read without mistakes and in my head.

Early years

Teenage years

Nowadays
My first book was a book of Disney fairytales. It still holds a special place in my heart. Right after kindergarten I got into the Harry Potter books, Chamber of The Secret being my favourite. Throughout my teenage years I read tons of books about love, mystery and the paranormal. Most of the stories were really dramatic and tragic so that was probably where I got my more theatrical side. I remember my favorites being the Twilight Saga and most books about vampires. When I first discovered Stephen King and horror literature, I thought that I should suddenly stop reading books meant for teenagers. To me it seemed like it was the beginning of a new era and that I should at least try and be an adult. This was a mistake though and it burned me so out that I stopped reading for a year altogether. When I finally got back into it, I ironically started with a teenage fantasy novel named Throne of Glass. This got me back into reading and reminded me that reading is for my own enjoyment and not for impressings others.
